
We're stupid, and
we'll die soon. 
Her remains lie
lifeless
as she ever was,
living only 
when no one was there to watch. 
I am alone
in my stupor,
in a mist. 
A thin mist through which I can see
the San Francisco Skyline. 
Impaled like Christ or a boar,
we bleed through our skins, 
howling like the wind or 
wolves. 

The water in vases scatters light
onto the walls, reminds me 
that the things I say have never been said, 
will never be said again. 
A fish jumps, splashes,
is forgotten. 
Things just below the surface
might as well be leagues down; 
lost forever. 

Words are 
incomplete, are 
immaterial 
Words are 
waves in the air, pixels
on the screen, pigments. 
Words are 
here and gone but
mostly gone. Here for a 
moment only. 
Like you, with your
premonitions.
