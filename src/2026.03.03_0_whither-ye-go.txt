And we were both there, in between
breaths. 
In between stolid sentences. 

We passed by a building with the inscription:
    
    And this land, whither ye go to posess it,
    is a land of hills and valleys
    and drinketh the water of heaven

or something like that. 

Whither or not we go to possess it, 
Whither or not we give it a name, 
Whither or not we traipse across it in 
a caravan of bikes or in a big black car, 
the land is of dirt and wriggling things
which drinketh the water of heaven.
And the water of heaven trickles through
the intractable surface area of the soil
and becomes the water of the earth. 

In between stolen sandwiches,
stolen glances,
half-baked ideas and premature
proposals,
In between adolescence and age,
the water and the Monterey Cyprus, 
underneath the bigger rocks and 
stuck between the cracks, 
our sense of self sat, camouflaged
like the sea snails and the
green-yellow crabs.
