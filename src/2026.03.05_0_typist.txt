
The veins pronounce themselves 
in the typist's hands, ballooning
like the blueberries in Ipswitch, MA
in August and September. 
Stretching the fabric, like boys in tight shirts
do to feel like men. 
Because a man is matter,
the taking of space,
the potential for violence,
and nothing else.
The typist scribes 
words of 
thinkers or of 
sayers, 
and what is the difference?

A person is an endless posturing,
a preening,
a relentless display of the self.  
Vanity is the only pastime, and so we go 
toward the world with razors bared,
flocking like peacocks,
like F-16s.
